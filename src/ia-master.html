What is Internal Auditing?

Internal auditing is an independent, objective assurance and consulting activity designed to add value and improve an organization's operations. It helps an organization accomplish its objectives by bringing a systematic, disciplined approach to evaluate and improve the effectiveness of risk management, control, and governance processes.

Internal vs. External Audit vs. Accounting

This table clarifies the unique roles of these three distinct, yet related, functions within an organization's financial ecosystem.

Attribute Internal Audit External Audit Accounting
Objective Improve operations, risk management, and governance. Provide an opinion on the fairness of financial statements. Record, classify, and summarize financial transactions.
Focus Broad: financial, operational, compliance, IT, and strategic. Primarily on financial statements and related internal controls. Recording and reporting financial data.
Independence Independent of audited activities, but an employee of the company. Completely independent of the company. Integral part of the company's daily operations.
Reporting To the Audit Committee and Management. To Shareholders and the Board. To Management and, through financial statements, to external stakeholders.

Professional Certification: The CIA Exam

The Certified Internal Auditor (CIA) is the only globally recognized certification for internal auditors. Administered by The IIA and often proctored by Pearson, the exam validates your mastery of the profession's standards and best practices.

Part 1: Essentials of Internal Auditing

Part 2: Practice of Internal Auditing
